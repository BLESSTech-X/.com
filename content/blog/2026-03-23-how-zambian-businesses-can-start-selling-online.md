## Why Now Is the Best Time to Sell Online in Zambia

Zambia's mobile money ecosystem is now one of the most advanced in Africa. With Airtel Money and MTN Money reaching millions of people across all provinces, accepting payments online has never been easier for small businesses.

Yet most Zambian entrepreneurs still rely on walk-in customers and word of mouth. This guide will show you exactly how to change that — in 7 days or less.

## Day 1 — Choose What You're Selling

Before building anything, get clear on your product or service:

- **Physical products** (clothes, food, electronics) → you need an e-commerce store
- **Services** (design, consulting, repairs) → a professional website with a contact/booking form
- **Both** → a combined site with a shop and service pages

## Day 2 — Get Your Content Ready

Prepare these before anything else:

1. **Photos** — at least 3 good photos of your product or team (use natural light, clean background)
2. **Prices** — decide your ZMW prices upfront
3. **Description** — write 2–3 sentences about what you sell and who it's for
4. **Contact details** — phone, WhatsApp, email, location

## Day 3 — Set Up Mobile Money Payments

Contact your Airtel or MTN business account manager to get a **merchant account**. This gives you a business number customers can pay to directly. You'll need:

- National Registration Card (NRC)
- Business registration (PACRA) if you have it
- A Zambian phone number

Once live, share your new site on:

- Your WhatsApp status and groups
